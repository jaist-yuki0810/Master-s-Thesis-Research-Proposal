Master's Thesis Research Proposal

Research on Data Augmentation by Generative AI and Explainability of Machine Learning

Core Research Theme

The research theme focuses on "Data Augmentation by Generative AI" and "Explainability of Machine Learning (XAI)". These will be combined with the field of "Decision Support", specifically aiming to build a system to support junior maintenance and operations staff in handling system failures.

Proposed System: AI-Driven Incident Response Support System

To assist junior employees (1-3 years of experience) in completing a series of incident response tasks without veteran support.

Function 1: Incident Cause Investigation

Collect and analyze various information (logs, chat, past incidents, configuration data, etc.) to identify the cause. Challenges for Juniors: Difficulty in information gathering/handling, lack of understanding of complex system architecture.

System's Value Proposition: In addition to cause identification, output the rationale for decision-making, supporting logical explanations.

Machine Learning LLM XAI

Function 2: Status Reporting (Internal/External)

Automatically generate and issue appropriate reports (during and after incident response) based on the situation. Challenges for Juniors: Delays in choosing appropriate wording, communication scope, and reporting methods.

System's Value Proposition: In addition to automatic report generation, output the basis for generation.

Machine Learning LLM XAI

Key Research Challenges

The pursuit of reliability and explainability in AI utilization in mission-critical fields is key.

Subject 1: Verifying the Reliability of Data Augmentation by LLM

Background: Incident response data is scarce and diverse (unique incidents, new errors, rare errors). Lack of training data for ML models is a challenge.

Approach:

  • Generate simulated training data (impact scope, fault location, LOGs, mock chat, cause, etc.) from past data using LLM.
  • Prove to what extent the output accuracy of models trained on generated data is reliable compared to using real data.
  • Explore methodologies for generating mathematically reliable training data using LLM.

Key Issue: Evaluating the reliability when combining data augmentation, LLM, and machine learning.

Subject 2: Explainability of Machine Learning Model Outputs Integrating Multiple Information Sources

Background: In mission-critical systems, presenting the rationale behind AI decisions is essential.

Approach:

  • Perform "incident cause/countermeasure identification" and "automatic report generation" from various information sources (logs, design documents, chat, past incidents, etc.).
  • Derive not only correlations in outputs but also causal relationships and detailed rationales, making them explainable to users.
  • Utilize algorithms with high explainability (Decision Trees, LightGBM, Gradient Boosting, etc.) and consider models with even higher accuracy and explainability.

Goal: Construct a machine learning architecture that maximizes explainability.

Expected Contributions

  • Skill improvement and earlier productivity for junior maintenance and operations staff.
  • Faster, more efficient, and higher quality system incident response.
  • Contribution to improving reliability and social implementation of AI in mission-critical systems.
  • Provision of academic insights into Explainable AI (XAI) and data augmentation techniques.
